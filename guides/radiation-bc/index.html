Radiation Bias Correction Guide#

Background: downwelling vs net radiation#

Surface radiation comes in two forms relevant for bias correction:

Downwelling radiation is what the atmosphere delivers to the surface — shortwave (ssrd / rsds) from the sun and longwave (strd / rlds) from greenhouse gases and clouds.

Net radiation is what the surface actually exchanges after reflection and re-emission. For shortwave this is ssr = ssrd − reflected SW; for longwave str = strd − surface upwelling LW. Net radiation is the physically relevant quantity for the ocean heat balance.

QuantityERA5CMIP6Formula
Downwelling SWssrd (J m⁻²)rsds (W m⁻²)—
Downwelling LWstrd (J m⁻²)rlds (W m⁻²)—
Upwelling SW—rsus (W m⁻²)surface reflected
Upwelling LW—rlus (W m⁻²)Stefan–Boltzmann emission
Net SWssr (J m⁻²)rsds − rsus (W m⁻²)always ≥ 0
Net LWstr (J m⁻²)rlds − rlus (W m⁻²)always ≤ 0

ERA5 and CMIP6 models use different surface albedo and emissivity parameterisations. Correcting only the downwelling component (old approach: ssrd → rsds, strd → rlds) leaves the albedo-driven bias in the upwelling component uncorrected. Correcting the net removes this bias because the ERA5 net already reflects ERA5’s observed-constrained surface properties.

pyGETM accepts net radiation directly (alongside the downwelling-only and calculated-flux modes), making this the cleanest choice for future projections.

Sign conventions and correction kind#

VariableTypical rangekindReason
net_sw = rsds − rsus≥ 0 W m⁻²* multiplicativeRatio of like-signed positives; preserves the QDM trend as a fractional change
net_lw = rlds − rlus≤ 0 W m⁻²+ additiveSigned quantity; additive delta is physically more meaningful than a ratio of negatives

ERA5 str is negative (same sign as rlds − rlus) — no sign flip is needed. ERA5 ssr is positive — no sign flip needed.

Both ERA5 variables are stored as hourly J m⁻² accumulations. Convert to W m⁻² with scale_factor: 0.000277778 (= 1 / 3600) and resample: daily before the QDM correction.

Method: Quantile Delta Mapping on net fluxes#

ERA5  ssr  (calibration period, daily W m⁻²)           → ref
CMIP6 rsds − rsus  (historical, calibration period)     → hist
CMIP6 rsds − rsus  (future, SSP period)                 → future

QDM (kind='*'):  corrected_net_sw = ref_quantile × (future / hist_quantile)

The same structure applies to net_lw with kind='+':

corrected_net_lw = ref_quantile + (future − hist_quantile)

QDM preserves the CMIP6 model’s projected trend at each quantile while anchoring the distribution to the ERA5-observed climatology.

Output#

The corrected fields are written as daily W m⁻² NetCDF files with CF metadata:

Variablelong_namestandard_nameunits
net_swSurface Net Shortwave Radiationsurface_net_downward_shortwave_fluxW m⁻²
net_lwSurface Net Longwave Radiationsurface_net_downward_longwave_fluxW m⁻²

pyGETM reads these directly. No decomposition back to downwelling + upwelling components is needed.

Temporal disaggregation#

ERA5 ssr and str provide the sub-daily diurnal cycle of net radiation directly. The diurnal cycle of ssr shows a clear daytime peak and nighttime zero, consistent with the multiplicative (*) kind. The diurnal cycle of str has a small amplitude (dominated by the near-constant strd) consistent with the additive (+) kind.

Add net_sw and net_lw blocks to disaggregation.variables:

disaggregation:
  variables:
    net_sw:
      era5_path:     "/data/ERA5/kaj/era5_ssr_{year}.nc"
      era5_variable: ssr
      scale_factor: 0.000277778   # J/m² → W/m²
    net_lw:
      era5_path:     "/data/ERA5/kaj/era5_str_{year}.nc"
      era5_variable: str
      scale_factor: 0.000277778

The disaggregated output is at ERA5 sub-daily frequency (hourly or 3-hourly) in W m⁻².

Configuration#

Full annotated configuration is in config/bias_correction_example.yaml. Key sections:

# ── Variables ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
variables:
  - name: net_sw
    kind: "*"
    regridding:
      method: conservative
  - name: net_lw
    kind: "+"
    regridding:
      method: conservative

# ── Observational reference (ERA5 net radiation, single variable) ─────────────
reference:
  net_sw:
    source: local
    path:     "/data/ERA5/kaj/era5_ssr_{year}.nc"
    variable: ssr
    scale_factor: 0.000277778
    resample: daily

  net_lw:
    source: local
    path:     "/data/ERA5/kaj/era5_str_{year}.nc"
    variable: str
    scale_factor: 0.000277778
    resample: daily

# ── Historical CMIP6 (composite: rsds − rsus, rlds − rlus) ────────────────────
historical:
  net_sw:
    composite:
      minuend:       # rsds — downwelling shortwave
        source: cmip6
        uri:    "cmip6://MPI-ESM1-2-HR/historical/rsds/day/r1i1p1f1"
      subtrahend:    # rsus — upwelling shortwave
        source: cmip6
        uri:    "cmip6://MPI-ESM1-2-HR/historical/rsus/day/r1i1p1f1"

  net_lw:
    composite:
      minuend:       # rlds — downwelling longwave
        source: cmip6
        uri:    "cmip6://MPI-ESM1-2-HR/historical/rlds/day/r1i1p1f1"
      subtrahend:    # rlus — upwelling longwave
        source: cmip6
        uri:    "cmip6://MPI-ESM1-2-HR/historical/rlus/day/r1i1p1f1"

# ── Future CMIP6 (same composite pattern) ─────────────────────────────────────
future_model:
  net_sw:
    composite:
      minuend:
        source: cmip6
        uri:    "cmip6://MPI-ESM1-2-HR/{scenario}/rsds/day/r1i1p1f1"
      subtrahend:
        source: cmip6
        uri:    "cmip6://MPI-ESM1-2-HR/{scenario}/rsus/day/r1i1p1f1"

  net_lw:
    composite:
      minuend:
        source: cmip6
        uri:    "cmip6://MPI-ESM1-2-HR/{scenario}/rlds/day/r1i1p1f1"
      subtrahend:
        source: cmip6
        uri:    "cmip6://MPI-ESM1-2-HR/{scenario}/rlus/day/r1i1p1f1"

GFDL-ESM4 (3-hourly rsus/rlus)#

GFDL-ESM4 does not publish rsus/rlus at daily frequency; only 3-hourly and monthly are available. The local files at /data/CMIP6/ssp585-GFDL-ESM4/rsus.nc contain 3-hourly data (shape (251120, 37, 38) = 86 years × 365 × 8 steps/day). Add resample: daily to each component block:

  net_sw:
    composite:
      minuend:
        source: cmip6
        uri:    "cmip6://GFDL-ESM4/{scenario}/rsds/3hr/r1i1p1f1"
        resample: daily
      subtrahend:
        source: cmip6
        uri:    "cmip6://GFDL-ESM4/{scenario}/rsus/3hr/r1i1p1f1"
        resample: daily

CMIP7 note: direct net radiation#

CMIP7 may publish surface net radiation directly (candidate names: rsns for net shortwave, rlns for net longwave). If so, replace the composite blocks in historical and future_model with simple single-source configs:

historical:
  net_sw:
    source: cmip7                          # hypothetical source type
    uri:    "cmip7://Model/historical/rsns/day/r1i1p1f1"

  net_lw:
    source: cmip7
    uri:    "cmip7://Model/historical/rlns/day/r1i1p1f1"

The reference entries (ERA5 ssr/str) and the disaggregation entries remain unchanged — ERA5 already provides the net directly. The variables, kind, and correction sections need no changes. This is the main benefit of the net-radiation approach: the config structure is identical regardless of whether the CMIP model publishes the net directly or requires a composite.

Running#

# Correct net_sw and net_lw (all steps: correction + disaggregation)
bc correct --config config/bc.yaml

# Correct only the radiation variables
bc correct --config config/bc.yaml --variable net_sw net_lw

# Correction only (skip disaggregation)
bc correct --config config/bc.yaml --variable net_sw net_lw --steps correct

# Disaggregate from previously written BC files
bc correct --config config/bc.yaml --variable net_sw net_lw --steps disaggregate

# Diagnostics
bc diagnostics --config config/bc.yaml --variable net_sw net_lw
